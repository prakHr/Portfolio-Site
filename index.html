1998-2013: Lived in udaipur

Udaipur, often called the "City of Lakes," is a stunning city in Rajasthan, India, renowned for its romantic charm, intricate architecture, and vibrant culture. Nestled among the lush Aravalli hills and surrounded by shimmering lakes like Pichola and Fateh Sagar, Udaipur has a unique allure that captivates travelers. Its history dates back to the 16th century when Maharana Udai Singh II founded the city, crafting a legacy of regal splendor. The magnificent City Palace, perched on the lake’s edge, reflects a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles, showcasing ornate courtyards, delicate balconies, and timeless paintings. Udaipur’s enchanting palaces, vibrant bazaars, and traditional handicrafts celebrate the city’s rich heritage, while cultural festivities and delicious Rajasthani cuisine add to its warmth and vibrancy. Whether exploring the labyrinthine alleys of the old city, enjoying a sunset boat ride on Lake Pichola, or witnessing a traditional dance performance, Udaipur offers an unforgettable experience steeped in royal history and natural beauty.
